We are suing Character Technologies, Inc.

We (Fleshly Inc.) are filing a formal lawsuit against Character Technologies, Inc. for violating the General Data Protection Regulation (GPDR), specifically failing to provide users the right to own, control and delete their personal data.

We take user data rights very seriously. At Fleshly, we do our best to keep your data private, in your control and safe. We believe all users have a right to own their data, and are entitled to privacy. Your data should belong to you.

Our findings

In our independent investigations conducted with the help of GFT Media, we found that Character Technologies, Inc. was unable to provide users with a personal data export within 21 days, or in some cases, entirely, despite being forbidden under Article 20 of the GDPR. Data exports should (ideally) be provided without undue delay.

Furthermore, Character Technologies, Inc. has not been transparent regarding data usage and persistence with its users. They do not have a maximum data retention timeframe stated in their Terms of Service. This is not forbidden under the GDPR, but is a highly discouraged practice in data security.

Our cybersecurity team has reason to believe Character Technologies is collecting behaviorial and device-related information that is likely shared across third-parties after in-depth network analysis. We are heavily analyzing and monitoring which data is shared and how they could be processing it.

Character Technologies, Inc. has systemically failed to adequately protect the data of its users. And we're taking action against it.

What We Are Doing

We are working with the Irish Data Protection Commission (DPC), other Data Protection Agencies and oversight authorities to initiate legal proceedings under Article 83 of the GDPR., which allows data subjects to claim compensation for damages resulting from unlawful data processing.

Our claims will include a mandatory requirement for Character Technologies, Inc. to provide full, accessible data export to all users, especially users under the EEA, more transparent communication between its users, and possible compensation for users who have experienced harm due to unauthorized data retention/misuse.

Data is becoming increasingly commodified, which is against our company's values. We do not and will never share your data with third-parties, or exploit it. We must demand accountability and fight back against practices that hurt consumers like this.

What you can do to help

If you have used a service owned by Character Technologies, have any information to let us know about or experiences using their products, please contact us directly at fleshly.help@outlook.com. We will listen to your feedback and continue to investigate. Your input could help us build a stronger case.
